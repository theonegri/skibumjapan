Skiing Japan by camper van.
A practical field guide.

A guide is based on first-hand experiences of winter camper trips in Japan. No sponsors. No affiliate links.

Car camping is part of Japanese ski culture and increasingly embraced by foreign visitors.
On winter weekends, many Japanese skiers and snowboarders sleep in their cars or vans to catch early lifts or avoid long drives.
It’s one of the best ways to chase storms across Japan and keep your options open between different mountains.

Between 24-hour convenience stores, onsens, vending machines, and resort parking lots, traveling by camper van is surprisingly practical.

After a few winters traveling Japan by camper van, friends and strangers kept asking how it worked and whether they could do it too. Rather than keeping those answers in DMs, I decided to write this guide. What you’ll find here is just my experience — the small details that made the trips smoother and more enjoyable.
