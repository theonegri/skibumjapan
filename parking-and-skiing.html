Parking & skiing

Important

All overnight parking listed below is based on local tolerance. Conditions can change with storms, weekends, and resort operations (snow removal, parking setup, staff access).

Region

Japanese Alps

These notes reflect winter use patterns I observed. Facilities and tolerance can change—always read signs and avoid disrupting snow removal and morning setup.

Nozawa Onsen

Paid overnight lot with toilets and vending machines; a common, convenient stop near the village.

Parking & sleeping

  • Location: Parking Lot 2
  • Map: Google Maps link
  • Cost: ¥1,500
  • Restroom: Yes
  • Vending machines: Yes (hot drinks)

Nearby essentials

  • Convenience stores: 7-Eleven and Lawson (~5 min drive)
  • Notes: Day parking is free, not overnight.

Myōkō Suginohara

Free resort lot with restroom and hot-drink vending machines; plan supplies because options nearby are limited.

Parking & sleeping

  • Location: Main parking Lot 1
  • Map: Google Maps link
  • Cost: Free
  • Restroom: Yes
  • Vending machines: Yes (hot drinks)

Nearby essentials

  • Convenience stores: 7-Eleven (~10+ min drive)
  • Notes: Park near the lodge and the other campers to avoid interfering with early-morning operations.

Kurohime Kogen Snow Park

Quiet, self-contained overnight spot in the lower lot; no facilities on site and sensitive to morning operations.

Parking & sleeping

  • Location: Lower parking lot
  • Map: Google Maps link
  • Cost: Free
  • Restroom: No
  • Vending machines: No

Nearby essentials

  • Convenience stores: 7-Eleven (~10+ min drive)
  • Notes: Do not park in the upper lot; it disrupts worker operations in the morning. We were the only ones sleeping there, but other cars were parked overnight.

Hakuba Tsugaike

Simple overnight option with limited services nearby; restroom availability may vary in winter.

Parking & sleeping

  • Location: Parking lot 2
  • Map: Google Maps link
  • Cost: Free
  • Restroom: Yes
  • Vending machines: No

Nearby essentials

  • Convenience stores: Small local stores nearby (not 24h)
  • Notes: The stores are small (limited options) and not 24h. Restrooms were closed when we were there.

Hakuba Happo-One

Central Hakuba village parking with restroom access and walkable food; popular and visible, so keep it tidy.

Parking & sleeping

  • Location: Main downtown parking lot
  • Map: Google Maps link
  • Cost: Free
  • Restroom: Yes
  • Vending machines: No

Nearby essentials

  • Convenience stores: Lawson within walking distance
  • Notes: This lot is in the center of Hakuba village and is popular. Many restaurants are within walking distance.

Akakura Onsen & Akakura Kanko

Close-to-lift overnight option; expect morning plowing and stay clear of operational lanes.

Parking & sleeping

  • Location: 駐車場 parking lot
  • Map: Google Maps link
  • Cost: Free
  • Restroom: Yes
  • Vending machines: No

Nearby essentials

  • Convenience stores: 7-Eleven (~10+ min drive)
  • Notes: Close to the ski lift; the lot is plowed in the morning.
Operational reminder

Parking lots are workspaces first. Don’t block snow removal, access roads, or staff areas—especially overnight and early morning.
